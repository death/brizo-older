Brizo overview

Brizo - A Minor Greek Goddess

She was worshipped by the women of Delos (not the men, for some odd reason) as the Protector of Mariners. Food offerings were set out for her in little boats (no fish, of course). Brizo was (or gave answers to) an oracle who gave answers in dreams about navigation and fishing matters.


(Taken from http://www.paleothea.com/)

What is Brizo?

Brizo is a simple IA32 decoding and formatting (disassembly) engine. It currently supports 80586 (including FPU), MMX, SSE, SSE2 and SSE3 opcodes, and two decoding modes (16/32 bit). Some stuff are not totally complete, yet I decided to release it - to see how much attention it gets.

Well, this library may not give answers about fishing matters, but it sure gives hints about fishy matters (hehe). It also helps navigating between instructions - the Brizo::Decoder will give you their length. If you think that's a lame excuse for the name Brizo, you're right. But you didn't write the library so you'll just have to put up with this :).

Brizo wasn't written with speed in mind, but clarity and simplicity. It originally was written as a disassembly engine for a debugger.
To make it clear - Brizo is my first attempt at writing a disassembler, so if it's all wrong and evil -- tell my why.
